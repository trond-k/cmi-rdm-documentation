# Navigate ethics and approvals

Research involving human participants, personal data, or sensitive topics requires ethics review and often regulatory approval. Planning this early avoids delays once fieldwork begins.

## Norwegian privacy regulations

If your dataset contains personal identifiers, Norwegian privacy regulations (personopplysningsloven / GDPR) apply — regardless of where the data was collected.

!!! warning "Personal data"
    If your dataset contains personal identifiers, Norwegian privacy regulations (personopplysningsloven) apply regardless of where the data was collected.

## Key steps

1. **Determine whether you need ethics approval.** Research involving personal data must be reported to [Sikt (formerly NSD)](https://sikt.no/en). Projects involving medical research may also require Regional Ethics Committee (REK) approval.
2. **Prepare informed consent materials.** Consent forms should be written in plain language and translated into relevant local languages.
3. **Consider host-country requirements.** Many countries where CMI researchers work have their own ethics review processes. Identify these early.
4. **Document your decisions.** Record which approvals you obtained, what consent model you used, and any conditions imposed.

## Fieldwork considerations

- In some contexts, written consent is inappropriate or unsafe. Oral consent, witnessed and documented, may be more appropriate.
- When working with vulnerable populations, consider whether participation could put respondents at risk, even if data is anonymised.
