# Create a data management plan

A data management plan (DMP) describes how you will collect, organise, store, and share research data throughout a project. Many funders — including the Research Council of Norway (RCN) — require one as part of your grant application.

## Why it matters

A DMP is not just a bureaucratic requirement. It helps you:

- Identify storage and security needs before fieldwork begins
- Clarify data-sharing expectations with partners
- Plan for long-term archiving or deletion
- Anticipate ethical and legal requirements

## Get started

1. Check your funder's DMP requirements and any recommended template.
2. Use a tool like [NSD/Sikt's DMP template](https://sikt.no/en/data-management-plan) or [DMPonline](https://dmponline.dcc.ac.uk/) to draft your plan.
3. Cover at minimum: what data you will collect, how it will be stored, who will have access, and what happens at the end of the project.
4. Review the plan with your project team and update it as the project evolves.

!!! warning "Living document"
    A DMP should be updated as your project changes. Revisit it at key milestones — after fieldwork, when new partners join, or when the project scope shifts.
